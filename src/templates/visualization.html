Data Preprocessing

Rating Distribution by Care System

Analysis Summary

This visualization compares the distribution of ratings across three types of healthcare providers: Government (GOVT), Private For-Profit (PFP), and Private Not-For-Profit (PNFP) facilities.

Key Takeaways

  • Government Facilities: Show the widest variation in quality, with ratings spanning the entire scale from 1 to 5, indicating inconsistent service quality across public healthcare providers.
  • Private For-Profit: Demonstrates a concentration of ratings between 3.5-4.5, suggesting more consistent service quality than government facilities.
  • Non-Profit Facilities: Exhibit the highest median ratings, with a significant cluster around 4.8, indicating strong patient satisfaction in this sector.
  • Quality Gap: The distribution reveals a noticeable quality gap between government and private facilities, particularly in the higher rating brackets.

Payment Method Analysis

Analysis Summary

The box plot visualization examines the relationship between payment methods (cash acceptance) and facility ratings, revealing important patterns in service quality.

Key Observations

  • Median Quality: The median rating for cash-accepting facilities sits at approximately 4.0, indicating half perform better than this threshold.
  • Quality Range: The interquartile range (IQR) between 3.5-5 shows most facilities maintain acceptable to excellent standards.
  • Outlier Facilities: Several low-performing outliers (1.0-2.5 ratings) suggest systemic issues at specific locations that warrant investigation.
  • Consistency: The compact box shape indicates relatively consistent quality among mid-performing facilities.
  • Upper Range: The extended upper whisker shows some facilities achieve exceptional ratings, potentially serving as models for quality improvement.

Geographical Rating Distribution

Analysis Summary

This spatial visualization maps healthcare facility ratings across Uganda, revealing geographical patterns in service quality and accessibility.

Patterns Revealed

  • Urban Clustering: Higher-rated facilities tend to cluster in urban centers, suggesting better resources and infrastructure in cities.
  • Regional Disparities: Certain regions show consistent rating patterns, indicating possible systemic factors affecting quality.
  • Accessibility Correlation: Facilities near major transportation routes often show higher ratings, highlighting the importance of accessibility.
  • Rural Challenges: Isolated facilities demonstrate greater rating variability, possibly reflecting resource challenges in remote areas.
  • Exceptional Performers: Some high-rated facilities in unexpected locations may represent successful models for rural healthcare delivery.
